The Medway towns consists of Chatham, Gillingham, Rochester, Rainham and Strood. Due to the area's strategic location on the River Medway, the towns have a strong maritime history. [Chatham Dockyard]({{ site.baseurl }}/19c/19c-chatham-dockyard), established in 1567 and closed in 1984 was an important naval dockyard for over 400 years. It was defended on its approach by Upnor Castle, and later, Fort Amherst. HMS Victory which served as Admiral Horatio Nelson’s flagship at the Battle of Trafalgar in 1805 was built here. Today, the Dockyward is a thriving tourist attraction and used as a location in the popular TV series _Call the Midwife_.          
Thomas Waghorn, a naval officer, who was born on the High Street, Chatham, is honoured with a statue for his achievements in postal communications and for his ideas of developing a new route to India overland via Egypt, thus avoiding the arduous journey around Africa.

William Adams, a naval navigator, who was the first Englishman to reach Japan in 1600, was born in Gillingham in 1564. He had fought against the [Spanish Armada]({{ site.baseurl }}/16c/16c-spanish-armada) alongside Sir Francis Drake in 1588. In 1605 he was appointed a high-ranking Samurai in the court of the Shogun. He died at Hirado, north of Nagasaki in 1620.
