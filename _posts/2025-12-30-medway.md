The Medway towns consists of Chatham, Gillingham, Rochester, Rainham and Strood. 

{% include embed/image.html src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/kent-map/kmo-forum-images/main/aec571ab-3301-4b9c-8828-3972d2760b42.jpg" aspect="1.5" caption="River Medway" %}

[Charles Dickens]({{ site.baseurl }}/dickens) had a fondness for the Medway towns and referred to them in his work. Westgate in [_The Pickwick Papers_]({{ site.baseurl }}/dickens/pickwick-papers) and the Nun’s House in [The Mystery of Edwin Drood]({{ site.baseurl }}/dickens/edwin-drood-curated-walk/) are modelled on the Eastgate House in Rochester. 

{% include embed/image.html src="wc:Engraving_of_Dickens_at_Gad%27s_Hill%2C_Samuel_Hollyer.png" aspect="1.454" caption="Engraving of Charles Dickens in His Gad's Hill Study" %}

[Chatham Dockyard]({{ site.baseurl }}/19c/19c-chatham-dockyard), established in 1567 and closed in 1984 was an important naval dockyard. It was defended on its approach by Upnor Castle, and later, Fort Amherst. HMS Victory which served as Admiral Horatio Nelson’s flagship at the Battle of Trafalgar in 1805 was built here. Thomas Waghorn, a naval officer, who was born on the High Street, Chatham, is honoured with a statue for his achievements in postal communications and for his ideas of developing a new route to India overland via Egypt, thus avoiding the arduous journey around Africa. 

{% include embed/image.html src="wc:Chatham_Dockyard%2C_HMS_Gannet_-_geograph.org.uk_-_3470474.jpg" aspect="0.752" caption="Chatham Dockyard, HMS Gannet" %}

William Adams, a naval navigator, who was the first Englishman to reach Japan in 1600, was born in Gillingham in 1564. He had fought against the [Spanish Armada]({{ site.baseurl }}/16c/16c-spanish-armada) alongside Sir Francis Drake in 1588. In 1605 he was appointed a high-ranking Samurai in the court of the Shogun. He died at Hirado, north of Nagasaki in 1620.
